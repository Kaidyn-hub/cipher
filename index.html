The Cipher Chronicles 🔐

A tale of three mysterious encodings...

🧩 Chapter 1: The Base45 Whisper

In the age of QR codes and space-limited messages, Base45 emerged as a quiet savior. Unlike its cousins Base64 or Base32, Base45 compactly encodes binary into a set of 45 readable characters. Used in European COVID certificates, it tells machines what humans cannot see. But beware — decoding it requires precision, for every trio becomes a pair!

📦 Chapter 2: Crockford’s Clean Code

Douglas Crockford disliked ambiguity. In his realm, Base32 was reborn — stripped of confusing letters like "O" and "I". His Base32-Crockford used only safe characters: no case sensitivity, no confusion, and with error-detecting checks. It was designed for humans, not just machines — a clean, readable way to pass secrets between allies.

🏛️ Chapter 3: Caesar’s Legacy

Long ago, in Roman times, Caesar shifted the alphabet. By rotating letters by a fixed amount, he masked his intentions. 'HELLO' became 'KHOOR' with a shift of 3. Though simple and easy to break today, his cipher taught the world the basics of encryption. His legacy echoes in every shift-based cipher used in puzzles and CTFs.
